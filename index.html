Reddit Mental Health Trends in 2021

Exploring conversations across Anxiety, Depression, Loneliness, MentalHealth, and SuicideWatch communities.

Executive Summary

This visualization article analyzes one year of Reddit mental health discussions across five major subreddits. Through heatmaps, bar charts, bubble charts, and TF–IDF keyword exploration, the goal is to understand how mental health conversations fluctuate over time, which topics dominate each community, and what emotional patterns emerge.

Monthly Activity Heatmap

The heatmap highlights which subreddits were the most active each month. This gives a quick understanding of seasonal peaks and sudden drops in activity across communities.

Posting Volume + Average Score

These paired bar charts show posting frequency (volume) and average engagement (score). Together, they illustrate not only when people post more, but also when posts are more appreciated or helpful.

Posting Volume vs Score + Monthly Score Trends

The bubble chart visualizes the relationship between posting frequency and average score, helping identify low-volume but highly engaging communities and vice versa. The line chart shows how emotional tone (engagement) changes across the year.

Final Takeaway

Across 2021, Reddit mental health communities showed clear seasonal trends, medication-centered discussions, and varying emotional tones. These insights reflect how online spaces respond to stress, crisis, and support needs throughout the year.
